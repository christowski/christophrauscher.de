    <p><a href="https://fragdenstaat.de" target="_blank">FragDenStaat</a>, the NGO run by the Open Knowledge Foundation Germany, is initiating the Überbrückungsfonds (“bridging fund”). It helps organizations that are waiting for government funds due to late payments—a real existential threat to small teams and projects.</p>
    <p>Together with their team I created a small and dynamic visual brand as well as an informative website and social assets to collect funds. See the project for yourself at the <a href="https://ueberbrueckungsfonds.de/?pk_campaign=topbanner" target="_blank">Überbrückungsfonds</a> website.</p>

<div class="content-container container--narrow">
  <div class="content">
    <p>The key design motive are gap fillers—you know them from digital calendars, project planning tools, video and audio editing timelines. The Überbrückungsfonds literally fills the financial gap NGOs are often facing due to delayed payments. The visual language communicates those filled gaps through bold shapes and forward-pointing arrows, while applying a serious yet optimistic color palette.</p>

</div>

<div class="content-container container--narrow">
  <div class="content">
    <p>The site’s structure has three goals: 1—explain the need for the fund, 2—collect donations, and 3—provide a possibility to apply for the fund. While the landing page offers key information and direct call to actions, the donations and application page templates provide in-depth guidance and information.</p>
